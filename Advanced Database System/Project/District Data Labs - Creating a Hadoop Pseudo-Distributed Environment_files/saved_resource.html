                          Really interesting blog post. Thank you for that!
                          We've been working using this setup on our projects in the past, but soon experienced that it could take us that far. The pseudo-mode just isn't how it works fully distributed ... and I'm not talking about scale here!

                          That actually is the main reason why we started thinking about alternatives. We came up with our micro-clusters that we are building at http://bigboards.io. It is a true datacenter in a box: 6-nodes, 24 cores, 6TB, 12GB RAM and 1Gb ethernet. Power, uplink and you are off. We provide a dashboard too. But the nicest part is our library. It's like the appstore on your smartphone but than filled with Big Data and datascience stacks. Later on we'll also add public datasets and documentation/tutorials. With the click of a button, those packages (we call them tints) are downloaded, installed and configured for you cluster for you!

                          So, you can just jump in and do your data wrangling instead of managing infrastructure and environments!
